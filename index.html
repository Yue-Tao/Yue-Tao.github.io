Brief Introduction

  • Ph.D. on Software Engineering from Carleton University, Ottawa, Canada, (2006-2010)
  • Chief Research Scientist, Simula Research Laboratory, Oslo, Norway, (until 2023)
  • Full Professor, the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Beihang University (since 2023)

Research Interests

All of Tao Yue’s research contributions are fundamentally rooted in basic research within the field of software engineering, with specific research interests on:

  • Software Testing
  • Model-based engineering (MBE) and model-based system engineering (MBSE)
  • Uncertainty-wise software engineering
  • Quantum software engineering
  • Digital twin technologies
  • Autonomous driving

Scientific Projects (Selection)

  • **Testing of Autonomous Driving Systems (ADSs)
    • DeepCollision
    • EpiTester
  • Uncertainty-aware software engineering - resulted in 12 methodologies and tools:
    • U-Model (conceptually modelling uncertainty), formed the basis of the PSUM standard at OMG
    • UncerTum and MoSH (UML-based uncertainty modelling)
    • UncerTolve (data-augmented model evolution)
    • UncerTest, TM-Executor and ASUBE (uncertainty-wise testing of CPSs)
    • RuCynefin (holistic robustness assessment considering both shallow and deep uncertainties)
    • U-RUCM (uncertainty requirements modelling), uncertainty-aware requirements prioritisation with search
    • NIRVANA (uncertainty-aware prediction validator of deep learning models), PURE (uncertainty quantification for self-driving cars)
    • RISE-DT (leveraging uncertainty quantification in digital twins)
    • …
  • Model-based product line engineering of CPSs and IoTs - resulted in more than 10 methodologies and tools, including:
    • Zen-CC (configuration conformance checking)
    • Zen-FIX (search-based nonconformity resolving)
    • Search-based test case implantation for testing untested configurations
    • Zen-DO (search-based configuration decision ordering)
    • SBRM (mining configuration rules with search and ML)
    • SBCR (search-based recommendations of faulty configurations)
    • SBORA (refactoring constraints with search)
    • …
  • Zen-RUCM combines natural language processing with MBE to enable requirements modelling, automate test generation, and so forth.
    • Some concepts of Zen-RUCM have been adopted by the SysML v2 roadmap2 and the UTP v2 standard, and the methodologies and tools have been used by several international universities to teach hundreds of undergraduate and graduate students.
